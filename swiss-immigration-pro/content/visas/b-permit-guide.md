# Swiss B Permit: Complete Guide to Long-term Residence

The Swiss B permit (Aufenthaltsbewilligung B) is the most common long-term residence permit for employed persons in Switzerland. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know.

## What is the B Permit?

The B permit allows you to live and work in Switzerland for up to 5 years, renewable indefinitely as long as you maintain employment and meet integration requirements. It's the standard permit for long-term employment.

## Key Characteristics

- **Duration**: 5 years (renewable)
- **Quota System**: Subject to annual quotas for non-EU/EFTA citizens (4,500 permits in 2025)
- **Processing Time**: 8-12 weeks for non-EU citizens, 2-4 weeks for EU/EFTA citizens
- **Renewal**: Indefinite renewal every 5 years
- **Path to Citizenship**: After 10 years, eligible for C permit (permanent residence)

## Requirements

### Basic Eligibility

1. **Employment Contract**
   - Unlimited contract OR minimum 5 years
   - From Swiss employer
   - Appropriate for your qualifications

2. **Salary Requirements**
   - Minimum CHF 90,000 (varies by canton)
   - Recommended: CHF 100,000+ for competitive applications
   - Must meet cantonal benchmarks

3. **Integration Requirements**
   - Language proficiency (B1 level recommended)
   - Social integration proof
   - Participation in Swiss society

4. **Health Insurance**
   - Mandatory KVG-compliant insurance
   - Active before application

5. **Valid Documents**
   - Passport (15+ months validity)
   - Educational certificates (apostilled if non-EU)
   - Criminal record certificate

## Application Process

### Step 1: Preparation (3-4 weeks)

- Secure employment contract
- Gather all documents
- Get apostilles and translations
- Secure health insurance

### Step 2: Submission (1-2 weeks)

- Submit to cantonal migration office
- Employer submits parallel application
- Pay application fees

### Step 3: Processing (6-10 weeks)

- Cantonal review
- SEM approval
- Quota allocation

### Step 4: Collection (1-2 weeks)

- Receive decision
- Collect permit card
- Register at commune

## Timeline

- **Total Time**: 8-12 weeks (non-EU)
- **EU/EFTA**: 2-4 weeks
- **Processing**: 6-10 weeks after submission

## Renewal

B permits are renewable every 5 years, provided you:
- Maintain employment
- Meet integration requirements
- Have no criminal issues
- Continue health insurance coverage

## Path to Permanent Residence

After 10 years with B permit (5 years for EU/EFTA), you can apply for C permit (permanent residence), which is:
- Unlimited duration
- Not tied to employment
- Required for citizenship application

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long is a B permit valid?

The B permit is valid for 5 years and can be renewed indefinitely as long as you maintain employment and meet integration requirements.

### Can I change jobs with a B permit?

Yes, you can change jobs with a B permit, but you must notify the cantonal migration office and may need to provide proof of new employment.

### What happens if I lose my job?

You have 3-6 months to find new employment. If unsuccessful, you may need to leave Switzerland or convert to a different permit type.

### Do I need to speak German/French/Italian?

Language requirements vary by canton, but B1 level is generally recommended for successful integration and renewal.
